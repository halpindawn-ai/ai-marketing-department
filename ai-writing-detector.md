# AI Writing Detector

A markdown file that catches the patterns generic AI writing falls into. Load it as a Claude Code skill, paste it into a prompt, or read it yourself before publishing anything you wrote with AI help.

Based on [Wikipedia's "Signs of AI Writing"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Signs_of_AI_writing) field guide, compiled by editors who reviewed thousands of AI-generated passages.

## How to use it

**As a Claude Code skill.** Save this file as `ai-writing-detector/SKILL.md` in your skills folder. Claude will load it automatically when you ask to check writing for AI patterns.

**As a prompt instruction.** Paste the rules below into any prompt when you want Claude to review writing. Add at the end: *"Review this draft for AI writing patterns. Flag every instance you find and suggest fixes."*

**As a self-check.** Read the Quick-Check Reference (at the bottom) before publishing anything you wrote with AI help.

## CATEGORY 1: Inflated Importance & Grandiose Framing

AI writing constantly inflates the significance of mundane topics by connecting them to sweeping themes. This is the single most common AI tell.

### Phrases to NEVER use:
- "stands as a testament to..."
- "plays a vital / significant / crucial role"
- "underscores its importance"
- "marking a pivotal moment"
- "a key turning point"
- "watershed moment"
- "leaves a lasting impact"
- "continues to captivate"
- "deeply rooted"
- "profound heritage"
- "steadfast dedication"
- "solidifies [its/their] [position/place/role]"
- "a broader movement"
- "significant shift toward"
- "part of a broader [trend/movement/effort]"
- "reshaping the landscape"
- "redefining the [landscape/future/industry]"
- "at the forefront of"
- "This isn't just about X, it's about Y"

### The "-ing" suffix pattern (superficial analysis):
AI loves to tack on present participle phrases that add fake depth. Watch for sentences ending with:
- "...ensuring [vague benefit]"
- "...highlighting [obvious thing]"
- "...emphasizing [the significance of...]"
- "...reflecting [the continued relevance of...]"
- "...underscoring [the importance of...]"
- "...showcasing [its/their commitment to...]"
- "...demonstrating [vague positive quality]"
- "...paving the way for..."
- "...enabling [organizations/teams] to..."

**What to do instead:** State facts plainly. Let the reader decide what's important. If something really is significant, show it with data or specifics, don't announce it.

## CATEGORY 2: Promotional & Travel-Brochure Language

AI drifts into tourism-ad tone, especially around culture, places, organizations, and products.

### Phrases to NEVER use:
- "rich cultural heritage"
- "rich history"
- "rich cultural tapestry"
- "breathtaking"
- "stunning natural beauty"
- "must-visit" / "must-see"
- "enduring legacy" / "lasting legacy"
- "vibrant [community/culture/ecosystem]"
- "dynamic hub"
- "captivates both [X] and [Y] alike"
- "nestled in"
- "boasts" (as in "the city boasts...")
- "gem" (as in "hidden gem")
- "world-class"
- "cutting-edge"
- "state-of-the-art"
- "innovative solutions"
- "seamless [experience/integration]"
- "robust [platform/solution/framework]"
- "comprehensive [suite/solution/approach]"
- "empowering [people/organizations] to..."
- "transformative [impact/approach/journey]"
- "holistic approach"

**What to do instead:** Be specific. Instead of "breathtaking views," describe what you actually see. Instead of "innovative solutions," name the specific feature and what it does.

## CATEGORY 3: Overused Connector Words & Transitions

AI leans on a tiny set of conjunctive adverbs far more heavily than human writers.

### Words/phrases to use sparingly or avoid:
- "Moreover" (the #1 AI tell transition word)
- "Furthermore"
- "Additionally"
- "In addition"
- "On the other hand"
- "In contrast"
- "Notably"
- "Importantly"
- "Specifically"
- "Ultimately"
- "Consequently"
- "Nevertheless"
- "Conversely"

### Section-ending summaries to NEVER use:
- "Overall, ..."
- "In summary, ..."
- "In conclusion, ..."
- "Taken together, ..."

**What to do instead:** Vary your transitions. Use shorter, more natural connectors: "But," "And," "Still," "So," "Yet," "That said." Or just start the next sentence. Readers can follow logical flow without a transition word in every paragraph.

---

## CATEGORY 4: Negative Parallelism / "Not Just X, But Y" Structures

One of the most distinctive and frequent AI patterns. AI loves to create dramatic contrast using negation.

### Structures to NEVER use:
- "It's not just about X, it's about Y"
- "Not only X, but also Y"
- "X isn't just Y; it's Z"
- "This goes beyond X, it's Y"
- "[Subject] is more than just X"
- "It's not X. It's Y." (two-sentence version)

**What to do instead:** Just state what something IS. Don't set up a straw man to knock down. If there are multiple aspects, list them directly.

---

## CATEGORY 5: Vague Attribution & Weasel Words

AI attributes claims to unnamed authorities to sound credible without actually citing anything.

### Phrases to NEVER use:
- "Industry reports suggest..."
- "Experts agree..."
- "Observers have cited..."
- "Some critics argue..."
- "Many professionals believe..."
- "Studies have shown..." (without citing a specific study)
- "Research indicates..." (without naming the research)
- "According to industry experts..."
- "It is widely recognized that..."

**What to do instead:** Either cite a specific source or state the claim directly and let it stand on its own merits.

---

## CATEGORY 6: Editorializing & Meta-Commentary

AI inserts opinions and meta-awareness of its own writing process.

### Phrases to NEVER use:
- "It's important to note that..."
- "It's worth noting/mentioning that..."
- "It is worth remembering that..."
- "It is important to consider..."
- "No discussion would be complete without..."
- "One of the most important things to consider is..."
- "In this article, we will explore..."
- "Let's dive in" / "Let's delve into"
- "Let's explore..."
- "Let's take a closer look at..."
- "In today's [competitive/digital/fast-paced] [landscape/world/environment]..."

**What to do instead:** Just state the information directly. Don't narrate the act of writing or reading. Don't warm up, get to the point.

---

## CATEGORY 7: AI-Favorite Vocabulary

Certain words appear at dramatically higher rates in AI text than human text.

### Words to avoid or use very sparingly:
- "delve" / "delves into"
- "tapestry"
- "landscape" (metaphorical)
- "intricate" / "intricacies"
- "pivotal"
- "underscore" (as a verb)
- "foster" / "fostering"
- "beacon"
- "realm"
- "testament"
- "meticulous" / "meticulously"
- "bolster" / "bolstered"
- "garner"
- "nuanced"
- "multifaceted"
- "cornerstone"
- "spearhead"
- "catalyze" / "catalyst"
- "harness" (as a verb, e.g., "harness the power of")
- "facilitate"
- "illuminate"
- "leverage" (as a verb)
- "navigate" (metaphorical, e.g., "navigate the complexities of")
- "crucial"
- "endeavor"
- "advent" (as in "with the advent of AI")
- "myriad"
- "paramount"
- "interplay"
- "synergy"

**What to do instead:** Use plain, direct words. "Important" instead of "crucial" or "pivotal." "Show" instead of "illuminate." "Use" instead of "leverage" or "harness." "Help" instead of "facilitate."

## CATEGORY 8: Formatting Tells

### Patterns to avoid:
- **Excessive em dashes (—).** AI uses em dashes far more than humans, and in places where commas or parentheses would be more natural. Use commas and parentheses instead.
- **Bold + bullet point lists with restated explanations.** The pattern of "**Bold Title:** Then a sentence restating what the bold title already said" is a dead giveaway. If you must use bullets, don't bold a keyword and then restate it.
- **Uniform paragraph length.** AI produces paragraphs of nearly identical size. Vary paragraph length naturally.
- **Uniform sentence length.** AI sentences tend to be roughly the same length. Mix short and long sentences.
- **Excessive bolding.** Don't bold for emphasis every few sentences. Save bold for truly important terms or headings.
- **Numbered lists for everything.** Not every piece of information needs to be a numbered list.

---

## CATEGORY 9: False Ranges & Empty Parallelism

AI constructs fake spectrums to sound comprehensive.

### Structures to NEVER use:
- "From [X] to [Y]" when X and Y aren't actually endpoints of a spectrum
  - "From intimate gatherings to global movements"
  - "From technical expertise to creative vision"
  - "From small businesses to enterprise organizations"
- "Whether you're [X] or [Y]" (false inclusivity)
- "Both [X] and [Y]" when the pairing is obvious or meaningless

**What to do instead:** Be specific about your actual audience or scope. Don't fake comprehensiveness.

---

## CATEGORY 10: Structural Patterns

### Avoid these structural habits:
- **Topic sentence then supporting evidence then summary statement** in every single paragraph (rigid consistency).
- **Starting multiple consecutive paragraphs the same way** (e.g., all starting with "The...").
- **Compulsive summarization.** Restating what was just said at the end of every section, even short ones.
- **Three-part lists in every sentence.** AI loves grouping things in threes even when the items are forced.
- **Perfect logical flow with zero tangents or personality.** Real human writing has slight digressions, asides, and personality. Pure AI writing feels like an outline that was expanded.

## Quick-Check Reference

Before finalizing any written content, scan for:

1. Any phrase from the "NEVER use" lists above
2. More than one em dash per paragraph
3. "Moreover," "Furthermore," or "Additionally" appearing anywhere
4. Any sentence ending in "...ensuring," "...highlighting," "...emphasizing," etc.
5. "Not just X, but Y" or "It's not about X, it's about Y" structures
6. Metaphorical use of "landscape," "tapestry," "beacon," "realm," or "journey"
7. Vague attributions without specific sources
8. Bullet points with bold labels followed by a sentence restating the label
9. Every paragraph being the same length
10. "In today's [adjective] [noun]..." openings
11. "Delve," "foster," "underscore," "pivotal," "meticulous," "bolster," or "garner"
12. Section-ending summaries ("Overall," "In summary," "In conclusion")
13. False ranges ("From X to Y")
14. "Seamless," "robust," "comprehensive," "cutting-edge," "innovative," "holistic"
15. Promotional tourism-ad tone for any topic

## What Good Human Writing Looks Like

Instead of avoiding patterns, aim for these qualities:

- **Specific over general.** Name the thing, cite the number, describe the detail.
- **Short sentences mixed with long ones.** Rhythm matters. Break up sameness.
- **Plain vocabulary.** "Use" not "leverage." "Help" not "facilitate." "Show" not "illuminate."
- **Personality and point of view.** Take a stance. Have an opinion. Include a specific observation that only someone who actually knows the topic would make.
- **Natural connectors.** "But," "And," "Still," "So." Or no connector at all.
- **Imperfect structure.** Not every paragraph needs a topic sentence. Not every section needs a summary. Tangents are human.
- **Active voice, concrete subjects.** "We reduced churn by 12%" not "A significant reduction in churn was achieved."
